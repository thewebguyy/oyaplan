# OyaPlan — Launch Playbook

> **Status:** Approved product source of truth. See `docs/product/README.md` for how this document relates to `CLAUDE.md` and other repository documentation.

## Week-by-Week Execution Plan

This is the operations manual for the six weeks before launch and the four weeks after. It translates the milestones already defined in the Operations & Growth Roadmap and MVP Scope into an actual weekly checklist — who does what, by when. Nothing here introduces new strategy; it's the execution layer underneath the GTM Strategy and MVP Scope documents.

## 1. Roles Going In

| Role | Owns |
|---|---|
| CEO (Engineering) | Product build, bug fixes, data model, technical readiness of Milestone 0.5–1 features. |
| COO (Strategy & Ops) | Venue verification, ambassador and partner outreach, content, positioning consistency, tracking KPIs. |

Adjust freely — the point is that every week below has an owner, not that these exact owns are fixed.

## 2. Pre-Launch: Weeks -6 to -1

### Weeks -6 & -5: Foundation
- Begin venue verification sprint toward the Milestone 0.5 target (100+ venues, 500+ menu items, 100+ transport routes).
- Finalise and test the shareable-link plan flow (WhatsApp/direct link) — this is the core mechanic the entire launch depends on.
- Lock the hero section headline and subhead from the Positioning & Messaging Bible; build the landing page around it.
- Identify and reach out to the first wave of the "first 100" list (campus connectors, nightlife organisers, creators, founders, friends-of-friends) — this is a personal outreach task, not a broadcast.

### Weeks -4 & -3: Pilot Outings
- Run the 10 pilot outings called for in the Operations & Growth Roadmap's Phase 0.5 activities — real people, real receipts, real feedback.
- Use pilot feedback to stress-test the cost estimator before any public traffic hits it — a wrong estimate in week one of a public launch is far more damaging to trust than the same error in a private pilot.
- Begin content production: first batch of "true cost of a night out" TikTok/Instagram content, timed to start posting the week before launch, not the day of.
- Recruit campus ambassadors for the 3–5 target campuses; confirm their weekly targets are understood (sign up crews, not individuals).

### Week -2: Dry Run
- Full internal dry run of the onboarding-to-plan-to-share flow, end to end, on real phones, not just in a dev environment.
- Confirm phone/OTP verification is live and stable — this is the one piece of "basic account trust" the MVP actually needs working before day one.
- Prepare launch-day content calendar: what posts go out, in what order, on which accounts.
- Brief the first-100 list that launch is imminent — a short, personal message, not a mass blast.

### Week -1: Final Checks
- Freeze feature scope — no new functionality added this week, only bug fixes. Anything not already working doesn't ship for launch.
- Re-verify the highest-traffic venues one more time — a stale price on a popular venue in week one undermines the entire cost-certainty pitch.
- Confirm venue partner listings (free tier) are live for the initial 10–20 partners.
- Set up basic tracking for the KPIs defined in the Go-to-Market Strategy (plans created, plans forwarded to WhatsApp, signups) so day-one data isn't lost to a missing dashboard.

## 3. Launch Week

| Day | Focus |
|---|---|
| Day 1 | Announce to the first-100 list directly and personally, not via a public post first. Get the earliest plans created and shared to real WhatsApp groups. |
| Day 2–3 | Monitor every plan created for estimate quality; personally follow up with early users for qualitative feedback, per the founder-led outreach approach in the GTM Strategy. |
| Day 4–5 | First public content push (TikTok/Instagram) begins, pointing at the live product rather than a waitlist. |
| Day 6–7 | Review week-one numbers against the KPI targets; identify the single biggest friction point in the onboarding-to-plan flow and fix it before week two starts. |

**NEW — Launch-day discipline:** resist the temptation to launch broadly on Day 1. The GTM Strategy and Market Intelligence Report are both explicit that a curated first 100 outperforms an open broadcast; a quiet, high-quality Day 1 is a feature of this plan, not a missed opportunity.

## 4. Weeks 1–4 Post-Launch

**Week 1**
- Daily check on venue data accuracy — this is the highest-risk period for a bad estimate reaching a real user for the first time.
- Collect every piece of qualitative feedback from the first 100 personally; don't wait for a formal survey.

**Week 2**
- Begin campus ambassador activation in earnest, now that the core flow has survived a week of real usage.
- Ship the first round of fixes identified from week one's friction points.

**Week 3**
- Push toward the first 500 target — recurring "group template" prompts (Friday plans, brunches, after-work drinks) should be live per the GTM Strategy's second rung.
- Publish the second and third pieces of cost-breakdown content; confirm the weekly content cadence is holding, not slipping now that launch excitement has passed.

**Week 4**
- Full review against Milestone 1 targets (30% user confidence rate in estimates, link-based sharing stable) — this is the checkpoint for whether the product is ready to widen acquisition further or needs another cycle of fixes first.
- Decide, based on real week 1–4 data, whether the referral group-unlock mechanic (Growth Roadmap, Section 3.2) is ready to switch on, or whether it's premature without enough estimate accuracy yet to make the unlocked breakdown trustworthy.

## 5. Contingency Notes

- **If venue pricing data is challenged publicly** (someone posts that an estimate was wrong): respond quickly and transparently, re-verify that specific venue immediately, and be visibly willing to say "estimated, not verified" rather than defending a wrong number — this protects the core trust proposition better than silence would.
- **If early signups are slower than expected:** resist the urge to broaden acquisition channels prematurely. The GTM Strategy's ladder logic still applies — a slow first 100 usually means the outreach needs to be more personal, not that the channel mix needs to expand.
- **If a technical issue breaks the sharing flow:** this is the single point of failure for the entire launch, since sharing to WhatsApp is the primary distribution mechanism — treat any bug here as highest priority, above new feature work, for the full launch period.
